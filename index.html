  • Casserole -

    Casseroles are a nostalgic American comfort food that first became popular during the Great Depression. The word "casserole" comes from a French word that means "saucepan" and the oldest known recipe is for macaroni and cheese, which dates back to around 1250.

  • Medovik -

    Medovik, a Russian layer cake made with honey and sweetened sour cream, was purportedly invented by a cook in the household of Tsar Alexander I who was seeking to win over Tsarina Elizabeth Alexeievna, who famously hated honey.

  • Verguny -

    Verguny is a traditional Ukrainian cookie made of fresh unleavened dough, fried in oil. Depending on the regions, the dough and shape of the cookies could differ. For example, traditionally the dough was cut into small rhombuses, then an incision inside was made and the dough was wrapped inside the incision.
